About ImmunoChroma

Revolutionary Point-of-Care Diagnostics

ImmunoChroma represents a breakthrough in portable diagnostic technology, offering a cost-effective colorimetric nanosensor that leverages gap-plasmon dispersion of self-assembled aluminum nanoparticles for antibody detection.

Our sensor achieves a broad dynamic range of 10 µg/mL–5 mg/mL with a limit of detection of ~4.23 µg/mL, ensuring reliable performance across physiologically relevant concentrations.

Key Features

Smartphone Integration

Built-in smartphone application processes user-captured sensor images and estimates analyte concentration without bulky equipment.

Colorimetric Detection

Tunable optical response in visible spectrum from structural configuration of gap-plasmon coupled nanoparticles.

Angle Independent

Unique angle and polarization independent structural color ensures consistent readout under various conditions.

Cost Effective

Low-cost manufacturing using self-assembled aluminum nanoparticles, making diagnostics accessible globally.

Quick antibody detection enabling prompt isolation and targeted interventions in disease outbreaks.

User Friendly

No specialized training required - empowers individuals to perform diagnostics in resource-limited settings.

Research Publication

Smartphone-based Immune Response Measurement using Colorimetric Self-Assembled Plasmonic Biosensor

Authors: Mahdi Soudi, Ángel D. Torres-Palencia, Caitlin Beech, Pablo Cencillo-Abad, Freya Mehta, Amir Ghazizadeh Ahsaei, Ishani Chanda, Pamela Mastranzo-Ortega, Javier Sanchez-Mondragón, Abraham Vázquez-Guardado, Debashis Chanda

Abstract

We present a point-of-care, cost-effective colorimetric nanosensor that leverages gap-plasmon dispersion of self-assembled aluminum nanoparticles on a phase-matched near-field cavity for antibody Immunoglobulin G (IgG) detection. The sensor achieves a broad dynamic range of 10 µg/mL–5 mg/mL with a limit of detection (LOD) of ∼4.23 µg/mL, ensuring reliable performance across physiologically relevant concentrations.
